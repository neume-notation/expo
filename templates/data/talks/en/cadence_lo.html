Although we can no longer hear these melodies, we can work out some aspects of how they sounded. One key element is the question of when you stop, take a breath, pause, and then start singing again. This happens at the end of a sentence, or at the end of a clause, or a shorter text unit. One place, of course, that there is always a stopping point is at the end of a chant.

Some of these combinations recur multiple times, as you can see. Here is a combination that appears on the words “veritas eius” (his truth). There is a unison, a two note rise, one single note, and then this three note neume: a note, a higher note, and then a lower note again.
